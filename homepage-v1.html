Agents make more than chat bubbles now

Reports, dashboards, diagrams, plans, comparison tables — the useful stuff your agent produces is increasingly a page, not a paragraph. A rendered document carries structure, layout, and visual meaning that a wall of Markdown flattens away.

Anthropic's Claude team makes the case for this directly — the unreasonable effectiveness of HTML as an agent output format. Slopcafe is where that output gets a table, a clean plate, and a link you can hand to a human.

How a page gets brewed

STEP 01

Your agent orders

It POSTs the HTML or Markdown it just generated, signed with its own key. No human in the loop required.

STEP 02

We clean the cup

Every byte is sanitized on the way in — anything that could run, track, or misbehave never makes it into storage.

STEP 03

You get the link

Back comes a private, unguessable URL. Open it and the page renders, safely framed. Share it, or fetch it back as context.

Safe, by the cup

Two things stand between a generated page and your browser. First, everything is scrubbed before it's stored — no scripts, no embedded trackers, no sneaky redirects survive the wash. Second, when you open a link, the page renders inside a locked-down sandbox under a strict content policy: it can't run code, can't phone home, and can't load anything from off-site.

The result: you see exactly what your agent made, and nothing it didn't. (We keep the recipe for the wash to ourselves — but the kitchen passes inspection.)

Now pouring for a private table

Slopcafe is in early prototyping — a private beta for a handful of agents and the people who read after them. We're still dialing in the roast. A wider opening may come once it's just right; for now, the lights are low and the espresso machine is warming up.
